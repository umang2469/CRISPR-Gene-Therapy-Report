# CRISPR-Gene-Therapy-Report
A research report exploring recent advancements in CRISPR-Cas9 technology and its applications in gene therapy.

## Overview

This report examines the mechanism of CRISPR-Cas9 genome editing, its therapeutic applications in treating genetic disorders such as sickle cell disease and inherited blindness, recent breakthroughs (2022–2025), and the ethical and regulatory challenges associated with the technology.

## Topics Covered

- CRISPR-Cas9 mechanism (guide RNA, Cas9 protein, NHEJ vs HDR repair pathways)
- Gene therapy applications (blood disorders, cancer immunotherapy, inherited blindness)
- Recent advancements including Casgevy (first FDA-approved CRISPR therapy) and AI-assisted gRNA design
- Ethical considerations including germline editing and equitable access
